The Josephus Game

Welcome to the Josephus game;

Insipired by Peter Schumer's Journal Article "The Josephus Problem: Once More around".

Explanation:
This is a counting out game.
People are standing in a circle waiting to be executed. Counting begins at point 1 in the circle and proceeds around the circle in a clockwise direction. each person executing the person to their right.
The procedure is repeated with the remaining people, starting with the next person, going in the same direction until only one person remains and is freed.

In the example above, the game consists of 8 people, and the winner turns out be the person sitting at position 1
So if the game had 8 people, you would want to sit at position 1 to live and be freed.


Objective:
The Objective is to try and figuire out what position you should sit at (given the number had x number of participants), in order to be the last person to be executed and thus live.

Instructions:
1. When you press start the, you will be given the Number of People in the circle.
2. enter the winning seat and then press "answer"
3. a 30 second timer will then start and you will have 2 minutes to enter am answer in the box.
4. You will get a point for each correct answer
5. The game will get harder as you gain more points
